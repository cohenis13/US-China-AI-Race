Methodology

How the U.S.–China AI Race Tracker measures each dimension — sources, proxies, and known limitations.

Adoption AI Adoption in the Economy

What this measures

The share of major listed companies in each country whose latest annual filing contains evidence of AI deployment, integration, or operational AI use. This is a country-comparable public proxy for economy-wide AI adoption among large firms — not a complete measure of all firms or all AI usage.

Why public company filings

Annual reports (10-K for US companies, 20-F for foreign private issuers) are legally required, publicly searchable, and consistent across companies and years. When a company discloses use of "generative AI" or "large language models" in its annual filing, it is describing operational reality — not just marketing copy. These disclosures are more verifiable than surveys and more automatable than manually curated data. The same data source (SEC EDGAR) covers both the US and China samples, making the comparison structurally symmetric at the data-source level.

Sample construction

U.S. sample (~25 companies): Major S&P 500 companies spanning Technology, Finance, Healthcare, Retail, Consumer, Industrial, Telecom, Energy, and Media sectors. All file 10-K annual reports with the SEC.

China sample (~20 companies): Major Chinese companies that file 20-F annual reports with the SEC as foreign private issuers — primarily technology, e-commerce, automotive, and logistics firms (Alibaba, Baidu, JD.com, PDD Holdings, NIO, Xpeng, etc.). These are the largest Chinese companies with US exchange listings. The full list of sampled companies is shown in the dashboard table.

How "AI deployment" is defined

Each company's annual filing is searched for deployment-specific AI language:

  • Deployment signal (positive): Filing mentions "generative AI" or "large language model". These terms are specific to post-2022 AI integration and reliably indicate meaningful operational engagement when they appear in an annual report.
  • Strategic mention only: Filing mentions "artificial intelligence" but not the above terms. AI is acknowledged as a strategic topic but without deployment-specific language. Counted in the denominator but not as "positive."
  • Not found: No filing found for the company in the search window, or the EDGAR API was inaccessible. Excluded from the adoption rate entirely.

Adoption rate = Companies with deployment signal ÷ (Deployment + Strategic-only companies) × 100.

Data source and automation

SEC EDGAR Full-Text Search (EFTS) at efts.sec.gov/LATEST/search-index. Queries are run per company using the entity parameter to filter to that company's filings within a 30-month rolling window — long enough to capture the latest annual filing regardless of fiscal year end. The script runs automatically daily via GitHub Actions and writes data/adoption.json.

What this metric does NOT capture

  • Companies that do not file with the SEC: Tencent, ByteDance, Huawei, CATL, ICBC, and the vast majority of Chinese firms.
  • Small and medium enterprises, private companies, or state-owned enterprises.
  • AI use disclosed only in supplemental materials, press releases, or non-EDGAR filings.
  • Verified deployment outcomes — disclosures are self-reported and may include early-stage or aspirational language alongside confirmed deployments.
  • Consumer AI usage (individuals using AI tools).
  • Sectors not represented in the curated sample.

Key limitations and biases

China sample bias: Chinese technology companies are disproportionately represented among US-listed Chinese ADRs. This makes the China ADR sample tech-heavy, whereas the US sample is more sector-diverse. The China adoption rate likely overstates economy-wide Chinese AI adoption. Compare directionally and with this caveat in mind.

Filing language: Both US (10-K) and China ADR (20-F) filings are in English, making search terms consistent. However, China ADR filings may use different language conventions to describe AI than purely domestic Chinese reports would.

Risk disclosure inflation: Annual reports often mention AI in risk factor sections alongside confirmed deployments. Our terms ("generative AI", "large language model") are specific enough to filter out most generic risk mentions, but some false positives may remain.

Frontier Models Model Update Activity

Measures public model update activity on Hugging Face Hub from a curated list of tracked AI labs. Counts new model versions published by US-attributed vs China-attributed labs in the rolling 12-month window.

What this does not capture: Closed models (GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini Ultra) are not counted. Labs are classified by country of the primary organization.

Compute High-Performance Computing Capacity

Aggregate HPL Rmax benchmark performance (PFlop/s) and system count from the full TOP500 supercomputer list, grouped by country. Updated twice yearly (June and November).

What this does not capture: Private AI clusters, cloud GPU farms, and systems not submitted to TOP500. China operates exascale systems not in TOP500, so its disclosed capacity is likely a significant undercount.

Talent AI Research Output

Volume of AI research papers from US- and China-affiliated institutions, sourced from OpenAlex over the last 12 months. A proxy for research output, not researcher headcount or citation impact. Papers with multinational authorship are counted in each country represented.
